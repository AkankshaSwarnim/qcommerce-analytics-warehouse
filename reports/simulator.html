Fill rate simulator

Fill rate = the share of a customer's ordered items that actually arrive. An order is only "clean" if every item does. This tool lets you feel why those two numbers pull apart — drag the sliders and watch a healthy-looking operation deliver broken orders to its biggest customers.

This page shows the maths for one basket size at a time. The dashboard reports the real averages across every basket (item fill 94.1%, order fill 66.9%) — which differ slightly, and panel 2 explains why.

This is where the argument stops being interesting and becomes a budget conversation. Fill rate is bought, not decided — with inventory, which is the one thing a darkstore has no room for. That is why "improve fill rate" is a wish, not a recommendation.

One honest caveat about this page. It applies the identity at a fixed basket size. The warehouse averages over a basket distribution, and E[(1−p)^N] ≠ (1−p)^E[N] — the function is convex in N, so by Jensen's inequality the true average is higher than the identity evaluated at the mean basket. Here: identity-at-the-mean gives 62.3%, the actual warehouse figure is 66.8% — a gap of 4.5pp. This page builds intuition about one basket; the dashboard reports across all of them. Plugging the mean basket into the identity and calling it the answer is a mistake, so it is flagged rather than hidden.
